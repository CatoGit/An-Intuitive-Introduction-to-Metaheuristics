Single-state algorithms

"All you’re given is a black box [an unknown function] [...] describing a problem that you’d like to optimize. The box has a slot where you can submit a [single] candidate solution to the problem [...]. Out comes the assessed quality of that candidate solution." Then you change the candidate solution a little bit and check whether the quality improves. "The algorithm will typically provide a selection procedure that decides which candidate solutions to retain and which to reject [...]." (Luke, 2013)
